1. Architecture (Hub & Spoke)

All spokes feed the Hub; the Hub feeds the accounting chain.

2. What was added beyond the original brief

Your spec defined 4 doctypes (Freight Route, Waybill, Trip Expense, Freight Trip). To make a full logistics business and wire in the money flow, the app adds:

  • Driver master — Own Employee (links to Employee) or Hired Contractor (links to a Supplier).
  • Transport Vehicle master — Owned or Hired (owner = Supplier); covers both Prime Movers and Trailers.
  • Logistics Settings — maps every logistics concept to your Chart of Accounts.
  • Trip Settlement — reconciliation of accrual profit vs. actual cash.
  • Freight Trip link auto‑added to Sales/Purchase Invoice, Journal Entry, Payment Entry.
  • Roles Logistics Manager & Logistics User, and a Logistics workspace.

6. Daily workflow (step by step)

Example: 30 Tons of cement for Twiga Cement, Dar → Mwanza, in a hired Scania owned by Mzee Trans Ltd, driven by hired contractor Juma.

Step 1 — Create the Waybill

Customer: Twiga Cement · Cargo: Cement 30 Ton · Loading: Dar es Salaam · Offloading: Mwanza · Status: Loaded. Save → Submit.

Step 2 — Create the Freight Trip (the Hub)

Route: Dar‑Mwanza (distance/fuel/allowance auto‑fill) · Prime Mover: the Scania (Ownership = Hired) · Driver: Juma (Hired Contractor) · Waybill: from Step 1 (Customer/Cargo/Weight auto‑fill) · Agreed Rate/Ton: TZS 120,000.

➡️ Total Revenue auto‑calculates = 30 × 120,000 = TZS 3,600,000.

Step 3 — Log costs as they happen (Trip Expenses)

ExpenseCategoryQty (L)AmountPaid BySupplier
Diesel @ MorogoroFuel2501,000,000CompanyPUMA Energy
Driver foodDriver Allowance–150,000Driver Pocket–
Mikumi weighbridgeWeighbridge Fine–30,000Company–

On Submit, each Company‑paid expense auto‑creates a Journal Entry, and the trip's cost & profit figures refresh instantly.

Review the live P&L → Submit (status → Completed). The Create ▾ menu now offers the accounting actions.

Step 5 — Run the accounting chain

7. Cost roll‑up & real‑time P&L

The Freight Trip groups every submitted Trip Expense into read‑only buckets:

Category enteredRolls into trip field
FuelTotal Fuel Cost (+ actual litres)
Driver Allowance (Posho)Total Allowances
Weighbridge Fine, Toll GateTotal Fines & Tolls
En‑route RepairTotal En‑route Repairs
Offloading Fee, Loading FeeTotal Loading / Offloading
Truck HireTotal Truck Hire
OtherTotal Other
Total Trip Cost   = sum of all buckets
Trip Profit       = Total Revenue − Total Trip Cost
Profit Margin %   = Trip Profit ÷ Total Revenue × 100
Fuel Variance (L) = Actual Fuel Drawn − Standard Fuel Allocation

These recalculate every time a Trip Expense is submitted/cancelled — even after the trip is already submitted.

8. Anti‑theft & cost control

On save, the trip warns in orange/red when:

  • ⛽ Fuel drawn exceeds the route's standard allocation (possible siphoning),
  • 💵 Driver allowances exceed the route's standard posho,
  • 📉 The trip has a negative profit margin.

Realistic benchmarks on each Freight Route turn these into automatic trip‑audits.

9. 💰 The Accounting / Money‑Flow chain

Everything below is created with one click from the submitted Freight Trip's Create ▾ menu, and every voucher is stamped with the Freight Trip so you can trace money end‑to‑end.

The invoice → payment chain, made explicit

REVENUE — collecting from the customer

  1. Submitted trip → Create ▾ → Sales Invoice → draft SI for Total Revenue, linked to the trip.
  2. Submit the SI → books Debtors Dr / Sales Income Cr.
  3. Customer pays → open the SI → Create ▸ Payment → Payment Entry (Receive). Invoice now Paid.

Both SI and Payment Entry carry the Freight Trip reference.

HIRED TRUCK — paying the owner

  1. Set Agreed Truck Hire Rate on the trip (only when truck = Hired).
  2. Create ▾ → Truck Hire Invoice → draft Purchase Invoice to the owner (Supplier).
  3. Submit it (Expense Dr / Creditor Cr).
  4. Pay owner: PI → Create ▸ Payment → Payment Entry (Pay).

HIRED DRIVER — paying posho/allowance

  1. Driver Type = Hired Contractor with a linked Supplier.
  2. Create ▾ → Pay Driver → Payment Entry (Pay) for the trip's Total Allowances.

RUNNING COSTS — auto‑posted

Each Company‑paid Trip Expense auto‑creates a Journal Entry on submit: Dr the category's expense account, Cr Accounts Payable (if a Supplier is named) or Bank/Cash.

💡 Books stay balanced. Every operation above is standard double‑entry. Logistics Settings only decides which account each side hits — nothing is posted "off‑book". The trip's P&L matches the General Ledger.

10. Trip Settlement — closing a journey

Logistics → Trip Settlement → Add, pick the Freight Trip. The summary pulls in automatically.

  • Enter Amount Actually Received (cash from customer) and Amounts Paid Out.
  • It computes Customer Balance Outstanding, Costs Still Outstanding, Net Profit (Accrual) and Net Cash Position.
  • Submit → status Settled — the journey is reconciled & locked.

It's the one screen that shows the whole invoice→payment chain and the cash reality of the trip.

12. FAQ

"Create ▸ Sales Invoice" doesn't appear? — Only on a submitted trip with a Customer and Revenue > 0.

No Journal Entry created? — Expense must be Paid By = Company and the matching accounts must be set in Logistics Settings.

Hire/driver buttons missing? — They show only when the Prime Mover is Hired or the Driver is a Hired Contractor with a linked Supplier.

Revenue field read‑only? — It auto‑computes from Weight × Rate. Tick Flat Rate to type it manually.

Costs didn't refresh? — Click Recalculate P&L on the trip.

Currency? — Company default (e.g. TZS); fully multi‑currency via standard settings.
